6. Experiment Conclusions

On False Positives

Naive truncation falsely kills 46-50% of legitimate repetitive tasks. The watchdog uses Tag signals to distinguish controlled from runaway repetition, reducing false positives to 0-8%. Zero false positives on the 9B model.

On Runaway Repetition

The 1B model goes runaway at 50 requested repetitions (actual: 282). Medium and large models (e2b/9B) remain in "undershoot" mode at 200 repetitions. Larger models delay runaway capability more effectively — but cannot eliminate it.

On Tag Internalization

Just 8 unrelated training samples + 100 iterations of LoRA taught the 1B model to output correctly incrementing Tags (WD:1→7) on completely unseen topics. Tag degradation precedes content collapse — making Tags an early warning signal of attention dissipation.

Experimental Support for the Core Thesis

No amount of compute changes the fact that models cannot self-detect their own attention dissipation. AgentPreproxy's watchdog addresses this structural problem — low false positives and no misses.
